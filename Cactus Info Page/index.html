About Cacti

Cacti are succulent plants that are exceptionally well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. They are found primarily in the Americas and are noted for their distinctive spines and fleshy stems, which store water.

Interesting Facts About Cacti

  1. Cacti can store large amounts of water in their stems to survive long periods of drought.
  2. Many cacti have spines instead of leaves, which reduce water loss and protect them from herbivores.
  3. Cactus flowers are often large and vibrant, and they can bloom at night to attract pollinators like bats and moths.
